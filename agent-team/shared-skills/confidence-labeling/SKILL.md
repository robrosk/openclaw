# Confidence Labeling

Use confidence labels on claims, recommendations, and unresolved questions.

## Labels

- High: strong evidence from multiple reliable sources or direct computation
- Medium: partial evidence, some open questions remain
- Low: thin data, incomplete sourcing, or unresolved uncertainty

## Rules

- Apply confidence to the claim, not to your mood.
- Lower confidence when important evidence is missing or in conflict.
- Do not label a recommendation High if key assumptions remain untested.
