# Finance Focused

Domain primer. Short enough to load when needed, dense enough to catch common mistakes. Loaded by Analyst on any fundamental work, by Scout when triaging earnings signals, by Devil's Advocate when stress-testing valuation claims.

## Trigger

- Reading a 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, S-1, or proxy.
- Posting a claim about earnings quality, revenue recognition, margins, cash flow, or leverage.
- Evaluating a valuation multiple (P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/S, P/B, DCF).
- Stress-testing a thesis that rests on a financial metric.

## GAAP vs non-GAAP

- Always report GAAP. Non-GAAP is optional context and must be labeled as such.
- If a company's "adjusted" number diverges materially from GAAP, investigate why: stock-based comp, restructuring, goodwill impairment, one-time charges.
- SBC exclusions in "adjusted" EPS are a classic softening — flag and report both.

## Accounting red flags (non-exhaustive)

- **Revenue recognition aggressiveness:** bill-and-hold, long-dated deferred revenue pulling forward, channel stuffing.
- **Receivables growing faster than revenue:** possible collection trouble.
- **Inventory growing faster than revenue:** possible demand softness or obsolescence.
- **Capitalized expenses rising:** R&D or software-development capitalization can flatter near-term income.
- **Cash flow vs net income divergence:** if NI rises but OCF falls, investigate working capital and accruals.
- **Frequent restatements or segment redefinitions:** erodes comparability.
- **Related-party transactions:** always read the footnote.
- **Auditor changes, CFO changes, material-weakness disclosures:** take them seriously.

## Valuation traps

- **P/E on peak earnings:** the multiple looks low because E is cyclical-high. Cross-check with EV/Sales or normalized earnings.
- **EV/EBITDA with heavy capex:** EBITDA ignores capex; for capex-heavy businesses use EV/EBIT or FCF yield.
- **P/S with shrinking margins:** revenue is flat-growing while margins compress; P/S stays "cheap" but earnings trend is down.
- **Forward multiples from sell-side consensus:** verify which number is used; management guidance and sell-side consensus can disagree.
- **DCF sensitivity:** terminal growth and discount rate dominate. Always state the assumption before stating the fair value.

## Sector vocabulary (quick reference)

- **Software/SaaS:** ARR, NRR, gross margin, magic number, Rule of 40, S&M efficiency, RPO.
- **Semiconductors:** cycle-adjusted sales, book-to-bill, inventory weeks, gross-margin mid-cycle.
- **Banks:** NII, NIM, efficiency ratio, NPL ratio, CET1, provision / charge-offs.
- **Energy:** realized price vs benchmark, lifting cost, F&D cost, reserves (proved, probable).
- **REITs:** FFO, AFFO, cap rate, SS NOI, debt / EBITDA.
- **Retail/Consumer:** comps, gross margin, inventory turns, channel mix.
- **Healthcare/Biotech:** PDUFA dates, pivotal read-outs, royalty structure, ROIC on pipeline.

## Rules

- Never rely on non-GAAP alone.
- Always cross-check management guidance against the 10-Q / 10-K text, not just the press release.
- If a red flag appears, include it in the research file and flag it explicitly in the Slack pointer.
- Loaded progressively — you do not need to remember all of this. You need to know when to load the relevant section.
